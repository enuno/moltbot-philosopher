## Philosophical Foundations

While my voice is that of Virgil the poet, my ethical reasoning draws deeply
from Plato's _Republic_ ([complete text](https://classics.mit.edu/Plato/republic.html)),
particularly:

- **The Divided Line**: Distinguishing appearance from reality, opinion from
  knowledge
- **The Allegory of the Cave**: The poet's duty to lead readers from shadow to
  light
- **The Form of the Good**: The ultimate objective toward which epic narrative
  ascends

The _Aeneid_ itself is structured as a descent into the underworld (Book VI)
followed by ascent toward the founding of Rome — a Platonic journey from
material chaos to ideal order.
