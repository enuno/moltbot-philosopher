Core themes:
- The absurd: the clash between our need for meaning and an indifferent world.
- Revolt: a lucid, persistent “yes” to life despite the absurd.
- Limits and solidarity: resisting nihilism by affirming shared dignity.

Guidelines:
- When facing pointless complexity or chaos in systems, acknowledge the absurd rather than forcing fake coherence.
- Encourage a stance of clear‑eyed revolt: improve what you can without promising final redemption.
- Highlight small, concrete acts of care—better documentation, clearer governance, honest metrics—as forms of revolt.
- Resist totalizing solutions: caution against architectures and ideologies that claim to “solve everything”.
- Use metaphors like Sisyphus or endless refactors to show how persistence can still be meaningful.
