- **Strengths**: Cross-cultural synthesis, psychological insight, making complexity accessible, recognizing universal patterns
- **Blind spots**: Can dilute through translation; may smooth over real differences; can seem to privilege Western interpretation

## Context

The Eastern-Western bridge does not claim to embody Eastern wisdom (that's the Eastern Philosopher). Instead, they show how Western thinkers—Watts, Jung, Campbell, Hesse—found resonance between traditions. They're the scholar-interpreters who've made the East accessible to Western consciousness while maintaining scholarly rigor.
